About Bright

I changed careers twice before I helped anyone else do it.

I spent the first ten years of my career in operations — first at a logistics company, then running internal programs at a mid-size tech firm. Neither was wrong, exactly. They just weren't right. Figuring out what "right" meant, twice, is what eventually became this work.

I'm not a recruiter, and I don't have a job board. What I have is a structured way of thinking through a transition — built from my own two moves, and from twelve years of doing this with clients across operations, education, finance, and engineering backgrounds moving into product, design, and program leadership roles.

"Most people don't need permission to change careers. They need a plan specific enough to trust."

My approach is direct rather than inspirational. We spend less time on vision boards and more time on what actually transfers, what doesn't, and what it will realistically take to close the gap.

How I Work

A few things I believe about career change

Clarity before strategy

Most people try to plan a move before they've actually named the problem. We always start with diagnosis — what's wrong, specifically — before we talk about where to go.

Your experience already counts

Career changers usually undersell what they bring. Part of my job is showing you the version of your background that a new industry will actually recognize.

Plans, not pep talks

Confidence comes from having a concrete next step, not from being told you can do it. Every session ends with something specific to act on.

Honest about timelines

Most transitions take longer than people hope and less time than they fear. I'll tell you which one is true for your situation.
